# V2.3 Visuals, Tables And Annexure Handling

## 1. Purpose

Define how visuals, tables and annexures should be preserved during manual Word packaging.

## 2. Why Visuals And Annexures Matter

Visuals, tables and annexures are often part of the evidence and usability of a client deliverable. If they are dropped or damaged, the document may still read well but lose its practical value.

## 3. Visual Handling Rules

- Do not drop graphics/models/pictures.
- Confirm image placement.
- Confirm captions/labels.
- Confirm readability after packaging.

## 4. Table Handling Rules

- Preserve table structure.
- Preserve headings.
- Confirm page-fit/readability.
- Confirm numbering and references.

## 5. Annexure Handling Rules

- Annexure naming must be consistent.
- Annexure references must appear in the body.
- Annexure completeness must be checked.
- Annexure placeholders must be used where content is pending.

## 6. Evidence Requirements

- Screenshot or packaging note
- Reviewer confirmation

## 7. Packaging Checklist For Visuals / Tables / Annexures

- Visuals present and readable.
- Captions and labels correct.
- Tables intact and readable.
- Annexures named and referenced correctly.
- Missing visual content marked `To be confirmed` or equivalent controlled placeholder.
